Biography

Welcome to my personal homepage, this is a great privilege and honor for me !

Z. Zhang is currently a Postdoctoral Researcher at Kyoto University, in the research group of Prof. Yoshihiko Susuki. He received the Ph.D. degree in Informatics from Osaka University, Suita, Japan, in 2024, advised by Prof. Yasumasa Fujisaki; and B.A. degree in Japanese from Changzhou Institute of Technology, Changzhou, China, in 2017; and the M.Sc. degree in Mathematics from Guilin University of Electronic Technology, Guilin, China, in 2020, under the supervision of Prof. Zhongjun Ma. From 2019 to 2020, he has been a Visiting Master Student at EECS, The University of Kitakyushu, Fukuoka, Japan, mentored by Prof. Masaaki Nagahara. His research interests include Koopman operator theory in nonlinear systems, sparse modeling, robust optimization, and control theory.
He is a member of SIAM, INFORMS, and IEEE.

The Story of My Name

My surname is ‘‘Zhang’’, which is a family name passed down from my (grand)father and ancestors.
My first name, ‘‘Zhicheng’’, is literally derived from Chinese interpretation of the proverb ‘‘Where there's a will, there's a way’’.
Such a given name signifies my family's hope for me to become an honest, kind, and persistent person.
Consequently, both my parents and friends usually call me Charlie.
